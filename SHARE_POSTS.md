## LinkedIn

I published a Google Earth Engine JavaScript template for screening Sentinel-2 imagery for candidate sea-surface optical anomalies that may be consistent with possible oil slick signatures.

This repository was prepared as part of the joint research project "Updating Reporting Methods with Advanced Technologies" between Hidenori Watanave Laboratory at the University of Tokyo Graduate School and Nippon TV.

The workflow uses COPERNICUS/S2_HARMONIZED, Sentinel-2 Cloud Probability, NDWI water masking, and the Oil Spill Index approach described by Rajendran et al. (2021). It keeps the Code Editor workflow simple, includes map layers and Google Drive GeoTIFF exports, and provides a Kharg Island example.

Important caveat: this does not confirm oil pollution. It is intended as a screening and triage aid. Independent validation with SAR imagery, AIS/vessel records, wind/current data, time-series imagery, and local or official reporting is recommended.
